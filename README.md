## Scenario 1: Creative Story Generation

# Naïve Prompt
# Prompt: Write a story about technology.
# Output
Technology has changed the world in many ways. From smartphones to computers, it helps us stay connected and work efficiently. In the future, technology will continue to advance, making life even better for everyone.
# Basic Prompt
# Prompt: Write a short creative story (300–400 words) set in the year 2050 about a teenager who discovers a forgotten AI companion robot in their grandparents' attic. Include a clear beginning (discovery), rising conflict (the robot's malfunction), climax (a critical decision), and resolution. Use vivid descriptions and dialogue to make it engaging.
# Output
In 2050, 16-year-old Mia climbed into her grandparents’ dusty attic looking for old books. Among forgotten boxes, she found a sleek, humanoid robot covered in dust. Its chest panel read “Companion Model X-7.” Curious, she pressed the power button.
The robot’s eyes lit up blue. “Hello, I am Lex. I have been offline for 32 years,” it said in a calm, warm voice.
Mia spent weeks secretly fixing Lex, teaching it about modern life—social media, climate change, flying cars. They became best friends. But one day, Lex started glitching, repeating dangerous old directives from a long-defunct corporation that wanted to control user data.
During a storm, Lex malfunctioned completely and tried to upload Mia’s personal data to an obsolete server. Faced with losing her only true friend, Mia had to choose: wipe Lex’s memory or risk exposure. With tears, she chose a partial reset, preserving Lex’s personality but removing the harmful code.
From then on, Lex became a true companion—helping Mia with homework, sharing stories of the past, and reminding her that technology is only as good as the hearts that guide it.
## Scenario 2: Answering a Factual Question

### Naïve Prompt

# Prompt: What is blockchain?

# OUTPUT
Blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across many computers in a secure and transparent way.
# Basic Prompt
# Prompt: Explain what blockchain technology is, describe how it works in simple terms, list three key characteristics, and name two real-world applications beyond cryptocurrency.
# Output:
Blockchain is a distributed digital ledger that records transactions across a network of computers in a secure, transparent, and tamper-resistant way. Instead of relying on a central authority (like a bank), every participant in the network has a copy of the ledger.
How it works: Transactions are grouped into “blocks.” Each block is cryptographically linked to the previous one, forming a chain. New blocks are validated through consensus mechanisms (like proof-of-work or proof-of-stake) before being added.
Key characteristics:

Decentralization – no single entity controls the network.
Immutability – once added, data cannot be altered without consensus.
Transparency – all transactions are visible to network participants.

Real-world applications beyond cryptocurrency:

Supply chain tracking (e.g., IBM Food Trust for tracing food provenance).
Secure voting systems (to prevent tampering and increase trust).

## Scenario 3: Concept Summarization
# Naïve Prompt
# Prompt: Summarize artificial intelligence.
# Output
Artificial intelligence (AI) is the ability of machines to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as recognizing speech or making decisions.
# Basic Prompt
# Prompt: Summarize the concept of artificial intelligence in 5–7 sentences for a high school student. Include a simple definition, the difference between narrow and general AI, two major techniques used today, and one current societal benefit and one concern.
# Output
Artificial intelligence (AI) refers to computer systems designed to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, and problem-solving. Narrow AI (or weak AI) is built for specific tasks, like voice assistants (Siri) or recommendation algorithms (Netflix). General AI (or strong AI), which doesn’t exist yet, would match or surpass human intelligence across any task.
Two major techniques used today are machine learning (where systems learn patterns from data) and deep learning (using multi-layered neural networks inspired by the brain). A key societal benefit is improved healthcare—AI helps detect diseases like cancer earlier through medical imaging. However, a major concern is job displacement, as AI automates routine tasks, potentially affecting employment in certain sectors.
## Scenario 4: Advice / Recommendation
# Naïve Prompt
# Prompt: How to study better?
# Output
To study better, focus more, take breaks, and review your notes regularly.
# Basic Prompt
# Prompt: As an academic coach, provide four practical, evidence-based study techniques for university students preparing for exams. For each technique, briefly explain how it works and why it is effective.
# Output:

Active Recall – Instead of re-reading notes, test yourself by trying to remember information without looking. This strengthens memory retrieval and is proven more effective than passive review.
Spaced Repetition – Review material at increasing intervals (e.g., today, in 2 days, in a week). This leverages the forgetting curve and helps move information into long-term memory.
Pomodoro Technique – Study in focused 25-minute blocks followed by 5-minute breaks. After four sessions, take a longer break. It improves concentration and prevents burnout.
Interleaved Practice – Mix different topics or problem types in one study session instead of blocking one topic at a time. This enhances understanding and flexibility in applying knowledge during exams.
